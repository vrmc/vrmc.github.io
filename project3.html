More about MobileAirQ

In response to the hazards of air pollution, several cities have put in place systems to monitor air quality. One measure done in Chicago, for example, is that they deployed sensors mounted on lampposts. Using “waggle chips,” these sensors measured several air pollutants. Chicago used this data to analyse and forecast air quality incidents. In addition, the city of Dublin uses its bike share programme to gather citywide air quality data. However, the data gathered from these systems are spread too thinly across the whole city. This makes it hard for pollution sources to be identified or for the effects of potential interventions to be analysed on a small, localised scale.



                                                           


There have been very few studies conducted with regards to air pollution in bus stops in Singapore. Hence, our study aims to gather and analyse data from many bus stops with varying characteristics in Singapore, in search of factors that would influence the PM 2.5 concentration in bus stops.The ESP32 board is designed for IoT applications, boasting low cost and low power consumption while providing inbuilt Wi-Fi and Bluetooth connectivity. Similar to a study on a wireless sensor network, the ESP32 was chosen from among its competitors due to its connectivity features and dual core, which allows data to be gathered from the sensors and sent to a separate location at the same time.


                                                         


We noticed that the number of air quality monitoring stations in Singapore is limited to a few locations, and the air quality listed is an average value from various stations and only reported in an hourly basis. We attempted to provide data on-demand by creating and designing a portable, easy-to-use detector. In the process, we learnt about the use of microcontrollers and 3D printing.
